Leech Carrier Encounter — RFC

RFC VoidCrewTerminus mod Design only

Design-level proposal for a new combat encounter — a hostile carrier that fires Leech Missiles which spawn small parasitic robots on the player ship's hull. Players must EVA to remove them. This RFC extends the Upgrade Forge's Sector Escalation system: the encounter gates behind a difficulty threshold and scales beyond it. For the technical companion, see leech-design.html.

Summary

A familiar vanilla enemy class (identified pre-flight) gets a new weapon: the Leech Missile. On impact with the player ship it does no hull damage — instead, it deploys 1–3 Leeches, small robotic parasites that attach to the hull. Most Leeches target a nearby module and degrade it; a smaller share chew through hull HP directly. The only way to remove a Leech is to EVA and hold a multi-tool on it, with periodic input prompts; missing a prompt causes the Leech to escape and re-attach somewhere else on the ship. The encounter is locked behind DifficultyScalar ≥ 2 on the Upgrade Forge's ForgeMeterController, and several parameters scale up from there.

Motivation

Sector Escalation (defined in the Upgrade Forge design) scales enemy numbers — HP, damage, loot tier. Two things it doesn't scale: encounter texture (deeper sectors still play the same combat loop, just harder) and crew role pressure (deep-sector fights still resolve at the cockpit, the guns, and the reactor). The TODO file at the project root names the gap: "lech — launched via missiles, attaches to ship and causes damage to ship until removed by a player like Star Wars buzz droids — small robotic spider."

This RFC turns that note into a coherent encounter. The aim is to:

  • Add a deep-sector encounter that forces crew to leave their stations mid-combat and EVA.
  • Use the Upgrade Forge's existing DifficultyScalar as the natural gating + tuning knob.
  • Reuse vanilla systems (hull HP, module repair, EVA, external observation tools) wherever possible — minimize new content surface area.
  • Reward investment in crew positioning (someone watching the hull) and point-defense layering.

Goals & Non-Goals

Goals

  • One new combat archetype that forces EVA mid-combat.
  • Reuse a vanilla enemy ship as the Leech Missile carrier — no new ship type.
  • Reuse vanilla observation tools (telescope module, cockpit 3rd-person, gunner external view) for crew awareness — no bespoke hull-status UI.
  • Reuse the vanilla multi-tool for removal — no new equippable.
  • Tie the encounter to the Upgrade Forge's DifficultyScalar; scale multiple parameters with it.
  • Host-arbitrated multiplayer via VoidManager ModMessage, matching the Upgrade Forge pattern.

Non-Goals

  • No new enemy ship type. (A vanilla class is patched to carry the new weapon.)
  • No new equippable tools. (The existing multi-tool removes Leeches via patched behavior.)
  • No internal destruction of Leeches in v1. (Killable-from-inside is a future variant.)
  • No bespoke hull-status UI. (Crew observation reuses vanilla tools.)
  • No persistence — Leech state, like all run state, resets on ship death.
  • No new loot or rewards from the encounter; existing combat reward systems apply.

Host Ship

A vanilla enemy class — identified during the pre-flight decompile step — is patched to carry the Leech Missile weapon at appropriate scalar levels. The Host is not a new ship: same model, same AI, same spawn tables. The patched behavior swaps (or supplements) its standard weapon with the Leech Missile launcher once DifficultyScalar ≥ 2.

Why reuse Building a new enemy ship adds an entire content pipeline (model, AI, spawn registration, balancing) on top of a feature that already introduces a new projectile, two leech variants, an EVA mini-interaction, and host-arbitrated multiplayer state. Reusing a known silhouette also gives players an instant "uh oh, that's the Leech carrier" tell once they've fought one.

Leech Missile

The projectile is half the encounter. It's the only counterplay window before Leeches land on the hull.

  • Homing with a finite turn rate. Piloting alone can't shake a fresh shot, but a hard maneuver can outrun its turning circle.
  • Interceptable by any weapon hit. Main guns, fighter mounts, point-defense — anything that connects kills the missile mid-flight.
  • Engaged by vanilla point-defense automatically, but the missile is resistant — higher HP and a fast terminal phase mean PD often catches it early-sector and frequently slips through late-sector. PD is a layered defense, not a free pass.
  • Distinct silhouette and audio warble — visually longer than vanilla projectiles, a steady audible warble (constant pitch, no escalation) — so crews can learn to recognize and prioritize them.

Spawn on Impact

When a Leech Missile hits the player ship, it does no hull damage. The impact deploys a number of Leeches, scaled by DifficultyScalar:

Scalar tierLeeches per missile (random range)Notes
0–1—Gate not met; Leech Missiles do not appear.
2–31–2First appearance. Cheap-hit minimum.
4–51–3Full range.
6+2–3Floor rises — no more lucky 1-leech impacts.

The 3-Leech ceiling is a hard cap regardless of scalar. Multiple missiles in flight, however, can compound — a 3-missile salvo across high scalar can put up to 9 Leeches on the ship before any are removed.

Concurrency safety rail Total active Leeches on the player ship are capped at 8 simultaneously. When the cap is hit, subsequent missile impacts trigger impact VFX and consume the projectile, but spawn no Leeches (the in-fiction excuse: the hull is saturated with electromagnetic noise from existing Leeches). This prevents runaway swarm states from soft-bricking a run.

Leech Variants

Each spawned Leech is one of two types. Type is decided deterministically by where it anchored, not by RNG:

Module-Biter (B-type) Hull-Biter (A-type)
  • If the Leech anchors within proximity of a module → Module-Biter (B-type). Locks onto the nearest module.
  • If it anchors outside that proximity → Hull-Biter (A-type). Locks onto the hull section it's on.
Why deterministic, not random Players can angle their ship so incoming missiles strike bare hull sections — and earn Hull-Biters-only outcomes for their flying. Random variant assignment would erase that skill expression. Position-driven assignment also reads correctly: when a Leech lands next to your engines, of course it goes for the engines.

Module-Biter (B-type)

The default variant. While attached:

  • Applies a flat effectiveness debuff to the target module (e.g. engines at 50% thrust, weapons at 70% fire rate). Removed when the Leech is removed.
  • Also deals HP damage to the module on a slow tick. Vanilla module repair handles recovery after the Leech is gone.
  • Multiple Leeches on the same module do not stack the debuff (a flat multiplier is applied if any Module-Biter is attached). HP damage from each Leech does stack.
  • If the targeted module is destroyed (by the Leech or by other fire), the Leech self-destructs — "mission complete" — with a graceful VFX/audio cue and no AOE damage.

Hull-Biter (A-type)

The rarer variant. While attached:

  • Deals damage to the hull HP of the section it's on on a visible bite cadence (~one chomp every couple of seconds, audible from inside the ship). Vanilla hull-damage and breach mechanics handle the consequences.
  • No debuff layer — the bite damage is its only effect.
  • If left unattended long enough, will breach the section by vanilla rules and trigger room depressurization. The encounter then layers a hull-patching emergency on top of the Leech removal.

EVA Removal

Leeches are EVA-only. Inside the ship, there is no way to engage them in v1. (See Future Variants for the "internal destruction" option.)

  • A crewmember suits up, exits via the airlock, and approaches the Leech on the hull.
  • They aim the existing multi-tool at it and hold to remove. Removal takes ~3–5 seconds (scales with DifficultyScalar).
  • While holding, periodic containment input prompts appear (see next section). Make them all → Leech detaches and dies. Miss one → Leech escapes.
  • Removal cannot be performed remotely. Cannot be done from inside through the hull. Cannot be done while the Leech is mid-crawl after an escape.
Why EVA-only The whole identity of the encounter is "stop what you're doing, suit up, and go outside." Watering that down — letting people shoot Leeches through the hull from internal turrets — collapses the design into "another HP-bar enemy that happens to render on the hull." The EVA commitment is the design.

Containment Failure

During hold-to-remove, the Leech tries to break containment. The player must complete brief input prompts (Void Crew's existing boot-panel-style mini-prompts) under a time pressure. Missing a prompt → containment fails.

  • Prompt count per removal: 1 (intro scalar) → 3 (deep scalar).
  • Prompt window: 1.5s (intro) → 1.0s (deep).
  • On failure, the Leech detaches, plays a brief "wake-up" animation, and crawls to a new random hull location where it re-anchors (re-rolling its A/B type at the new location). Damage pauses during the crawl.
  • One escape per Leech. A Leech that has escaped once cannot escape again — the next containment attempt resolves normally regardless of missed prompts. This guarantees the encounter ends in finite time.

Sector Escalation Tie-In

The Upgrade Forge's ForgeMeterController.DifficultyScalar increments by +1 per sector jump. This RFC adds the Leech Carrier encounter to the things that scale with it:

  • Gating. Leech Missiles only appear at DifficultyScalar ≥ 2. Players have at least one full sector to learn the rest of the game before this threat archetype lands.
  • Encounter scaling. Multiple parameters scale beyond the gate — see Scaling Reference.
  • Tuning knob compatibility. All scaling values live in TerminusConfig and are tunable without rebuilding.
Composition with the Upgrade Forge Upgrade Forge Phase 6 (Sector Escalation) and this encounter both light up at roughly the same scalar. They cooperate: deeper sectors mean tougher enemies AND new threat archetypes AND better loot. The "do we jump again?" decision gains weight.

Crew Awareness

The encounter operates on the principle that someone on the crew should be watching the ship. Awareness is a crew communication problem, by design — no bespoke alert popup, no hull-status console.

  • Visual awareness comes from existing tools: the ship's telescope module (external 3rd-person view), the pilot's 3rd-person cockpit view, and the external view available at gunner turret stations.
  • Communication is the binding agent. Whoever's on lookout calls out Leech positions to whoever's free to EVA. This rewards Void Crew's existing role specialization.
  • Audio cues are diegetic — bite/chomp/impact sounds travel through the hull and can be heard by crew inside the affected section. Acts as a secondary "something's wrong nearby" signal but doesn't substitute for visual confirmation.
  • One global cue: the first Leech of an encounter triggers a subtle ship-wide hull-resonance hum so even idle crew know the encounter has started. After that first sting, all audio is purely diegetic.

A Sample Encounter

To make this concrete, here's how a Leech encounter might unfold mid-run, at DifficultyScalar 4:

T+0s — Inbound

Mid-fight in sector 5. The pilot, watching from the cockpit's 3rd-person view, sees a familiar enemy frigate. A long, oddly-colored missile detaches from its launcher, audibly warbling. Vanilla projectile warning pings. Pilot calls "Leech missile, port side." Gunner pivots a turret — too slow, the missile slips inside the gun arc. Point-defense engages and lands a hit, but the missile keeps coming. Impact.

T+3s — Spawn

Hull-wide resonance hum — the encounter's opening sting. The missile bursts on impact and deploys 2 Leeches. One lands beside the starboard engine module — Module-Biter, locks on. The other lands on an exposed hull section near the medbay — Hull-Biter. Engines drop to 60% thrust. From inside the medbay, the crewmate on stim duty hears a slow metallic chomp from the hull above her head.

T+12s — Triage

Captain calls it: "Engine Leech first — we lose thrust we lose the fight. Hull Leech second." Crewmate suits up, exits through the rear airlock, mag-walks to the engine Leech. Aims the multi-tool, starts holding. After 2 seconds, a containment prompt appears — "Tap A!" — she taps it in time. Second prompt 2 seconds later — same, in time. Third — she fumbles. The Leech detaches, sparks fly, and it scurries off across the hull. Engines snap back to 100% — for now.

T+30s — Chase

The escaped Leech re-anchors at a new spot — happens to be near the cargo module. Re-rolls as Module-Biter, locks on. Cargo capacity drops to 60%. The crewmate, still on EVA, jogs across the hull to the new position. Holds the multi-tool. This time all prompts land. The Leech detaches with a satisfying pop. One down.

Meanwhile, the Hull-Biter has been chewing on the medbay's outer plate. Hull HP for that section is at 40%. If she doesn't get there soon, the medbay breaches and depressurizes.

T+50s — Resolution

She reaches the Hull-Biter. The chomp cadence is louder now — she can hear it through her suit. Hold. Three prompts, all landed. Pop. Section hull stops bleeding. Crewmate calls "Hull clear" and heads back to the airlock. Total downtime: ~50 seconds, no module loss, no breach. Engines and cargo will need a small repair pass after the fight to top up HP.

Open Questions

Deferred to playtest rather than locked at design time.

  • Which vanilla enemy class is the Host? Pre-flight decompile to identify a mid-tier frigate or similar that already appears in deeper sectors. Once identified, the patch surface is specific.
  • Does vanilla point-defense already detect custom projectiles? If yes, free integration. If no, we need to register the Leech Missile as a valid PD target.
  • Does the vanilla projectile-warning system pick up custom projectiles? Affects whether the "incoming missile" alert fires automatically or needs a patched-in announcement.
  • EVA tool interaction shape. Does the multi-tool already have a "hold to do thing" gesture we can hook, or do we need a new interaction state? Pre-flight check.
  • Concurrency cap value. 8 is the starter — playtest may push it up or down.
  • Containment prompt UI. Reuse the existing boot-panel UI element, or author a new bespoke prompt? Cheap to reuse; depends on whether the existing element looks right in the EVA HUD context.

Risks & Mitigations

RiskMitigation
Encounter trivialized by PD-heavy builds — missile never lands. Per-scalar missile HP and speed scaling. Late-sector missiles slip through PD often. If still trivialized after playtest, bump scaling more aggressively.
Encounter punishing for solo / low-headcount crews — pilot can't both fly and EVA. Encounter intentionally rewards crew. Solo players will lose modules and take hull damage — that's a known cost of playing solo at deep sectors. Sector Escalation gates the encounter so solos can extract earlier.
Containment prompts feel mashy / inputs feel disconnected from EVA fiction. Reuse the boot-panel prompt grammar players already know. If reuse looks wrong in HUD, author a bespoke EVA-specific prompt UI in Phase: Polish.
Leeches stacking on the same module → module destroyed before crew can EVA. Non-stacking debuff multiplier (debuff cap is one Leech's worth, even if 3 are on the same module). HP damage still stacks, so it remains a threat, but not an unrecoverable one.
Concurrency safety rail (8 cap) makes the encounter trivially defeatable late-run — fly the ship into missiles to "tank out" the cap. Each missile still consumes a projectile slot, so absorbing 8 Leeches' worth still requires several impacts. If exploit emerges, reduce cap, or add cap-recovery behavior over time.
EVA traversal across hull is slow → encounter resolves on a 60–90s timeline that drags combat. Per-scalar fire interval tuning ensures encounters don't overlap. Hold-to-remove duration is short (3–5s). If still slow, mag-boot traversal speed is a vanilla knob.
Multiplayer desync — clients see Leeches in different states. Host-arbitrated state, mirroring the Upgrade Forge pattern. All RNG host-side; clients replay.

Alternatives Considered

New enemy ship type as Host

Design a brand-new ship — model, AI, spawn registration. Rejected because the repo has no enemy authoring infrastructure yet; building it on top of the Leech feature triples scope. A patched vanilla class is enough.

Random A/B variant assignment

Roll each Leech's variant on spawn from a probability table. Rejected because it erases the skill expression of "fly the ship so missiles hit bare hull." Deterministic position-driven variant assignment also reads better — the Leech that lands next to your engines is going for the engines, not by lottery.

Internal destruction (kill Leeches with internal turrets)

Let players engage Leeches through the hull from inside. Rejected for v1 because it collapses the EVA-centric identity of the encounter. Captured as a future variant for a deeper-sector "armored" Leech subtype.

Dedicated EVA tool (Leech Pry-Bar)

Author a new equippable specifically for Leech removal. Rejected for v1 because the existing multi-tool is enough and adding a new tool means a new pickup, a new UI slot, and new tutorialization. Captured as a future variant.

Crawling-by-default Leeches

Leeches actively patrol or seek across the hull. Rejected for v1 because hull-surface locomotion on arbitrary mesh geometry is a significant engineering investment. Static-with-escape-on-failure preserves enough drama; mobile Leeches are a future variant.

Pure RNG containment failure (no input prompts)

Random per-second roll for containment break, no player agency. Rejected because it feels unfair in multiplayer ("EVA player just lost 30 seconds to the dice"). Hybrid hold + prompts gives players ownership of the outcome.

Pure input-sequence removal (no hold)

Removal is purely a button-mash mini-game, no hold period. Rejected because it loses the "committed to the spot" tension of hold-to-remove and trivializes the encounter into a QTE.

Bespoke hull-status UI / alert popups

Mod-side UI showing all attached Leech positions, plus on-attach alert popups. Rejected because the ship already has telescope, cockpit, and turret external views. Crew awareness should reuse existing tools, not be overridden by a mod-side cheat sheet.

Future Variants

Captured during design but explicitly out of scope for v1. Each is a meaningful addition once the core encounter is locked.

  • Internal-destructible armored Leeches — deep-sector subtype that can be shot through the hull with internal turrets, but with a steep cost (chip damage to your own hull). Re-introduces the "emergency button" without trivializing v1's EVA loop.
  • Dedicated removal tool — a Leech Pry-Bar equippable that removes faster or with fewer prompts. Adds a build-decision layer ("do we bring the pry-bar?").
  • Crawling Leeches with IK locomotion — full procedural spider-walk across the hull (Animation Rigging package), either random-walk or seeking. Requires hull-surface pathing infrastructure.
  • Defensive lunge on close range — Leeches snap at the EVA crewmember during removal, interrupting the hold or chip-damaging the suit. Requires the IK locomotion foundation.
  • Burrowing Leech subtype — fleeing Leeches penetrate the hull and become internal pests requiring a different removal mechanic. Effectively a whole second design pillar; reserved for v2.
  • Self-destruct AOE on mission completion — destroyed module victims trigger a small explosion damaging adjacent systems. Adds escalation flavor; gated on playtest feedback that the current graceful self-destruct feels underwhelming.
  • Custom audio / visual polish pass — bespoke Leech model, missile model, VFX, SFX. v1 ships with primitives.
