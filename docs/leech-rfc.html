Leech Carrier Encounter — RFC

RFC VoidCrewTerminus mod Design only

Design-level proposal for a new combat encounter — a hostile carrier that fires Leech Missiles which spawn parasitic robots on the player ship's hull. Players must EVA to remove them. Gates behind DifficultyScalar ≥ 2 from the Upgrade Forge's Sector Escalation system. For the technical companion, see leech-design.html.

Summary

A vanilla enemy ship class gets a new weapon: the Leech Missile. On impact it does no hull damage — instead it deploys 1–3 Leeches, small robotic parasites that latch onto the hull and degrade the nearest module (or chew on hull HP if no module is nearby). The only way to remove a Leech is to EVA and hold a multi-tool on it for a few seconds. The encounter is locked behind DifficultyScalar ≥ 2 and scales beyond it.

Motivation

Sector Escalation (defined in the Upgrade Forge RFC) scales enemy numbers — HP, damage, loot tier. Two things it doesn't scale: encounter texture (deep-sector combat is still cockpit + guns + reactor) and crew role pressure. The TODO file names the gap: "leech — launched via missiles, attaches to ship and causes damage until removed by a player like Star Wars buzz droids."

This RFC turns that into an encounter. The aim is to:

  • Add a deep-sector encounter that forces crew to leave their stations mid-combat and EVA.
  • Use the Upgrade Forge's existing DifficultyScalar as the gating + tuning knob.
  • Reuse vanilla systems (hull HP, module repair, EVA, multi-tool, external observation) wherever possible.

Goals & Non-Goals

Goals

  • One new combat archetype that forces EVA mid-combat.
  • Reuse a vanilla enemy ship as the carrier — no new ship type.
  • Reuse the multi-tool for removal — no new equippable.
  • Reuse vanilla observation tools (telescope, 3rd-person cockpit, gunner external view) for crew awareness — no bespoke hull-status UI.
  • Tie the encounter to the Upgrade Forge's DifficultyScalar; scale parameters with it.
  • Host-arbitrated multiplayer via VoidManager ModMessage, matching the Upgrade Forge pattern.

Non-Goals

  • No new enemy ship type. A vanilla class is patched to carry the new weapon.
  • No new equippable tools. The existing multi-tool removes Leeches.
  • No internal destruction of Leeches in v1. (Future variant.)
  • No bespoke hull-status UI. Crew observation reuses vanilla tools.
  • No persistence. Leech state, like all run state, resets on ship death.
  • No new loot or rewards from the encounter.

Host Ship

A vanilla enemy class — identified during the pre-flight decompile step — is patched to carry the Leech Missile launcher at DifficultyScalar ≥ 2. Same model, same AI, same spawn tables. The patched behavior swaps (or supplements) its standard weapon with the new launcher.

Why reuse A new enemy ship adds a full content pipeline (model, AI, spawn registration, balancing) on top of a feature that already introduces a new projectile, a new attached-entity, an EVA interaction, and host-arbitrated state. Reusing a known silhouette also gives players the "uh oh, that's the Leech carrier" tell once they've seen one.

Leech Missile

The projectile is the only counterplay window before Leeches land on the hull.

  • Homing with a finite turn rate. Piloting alone can't shake a fresh shot, but a hard maneuver can outrun its turning circle.
  • Interceptable by any weapon hit. Main guns, fighter mounts, point-defense — anything that connects kills the missile.
  • Engaged by vanilla point-defense automatically, but missiles are resistant — PD catches them early-sector and frequently slips late-sector. PD is a layered defense, not a free pass.
  • Distinct silhouette and audio warble so crews can learn to recognize and prioritize them.

The Leech

When a Leech Missile hits the player ship, it does no hull damage. The impact deploys 1–3 Leeches, scaling with DifficultyScalar (see Scaling Reference). Each Leech anchors where it landed and immediately starts working:

  • If a module is nearby, the Leech locks onto it and applies a flat effectiveness debuff (e.g. engines at 50% thrust, weapons at 70% fire rate) plus a slow tick of module HP damage. The debuff lifts when the Leech is removed; HP is recovered via vanilla module repair.
  • If no module is nearby, the Leech chews the hull section it's on at a visible bite cadence — a slow audible chomp from inside the ship. Left long enough, it breaches the section by vanilla rules and depressurizes the room.
  • Multiple Leeches on the same module do not stack the debuff (flat multiplier applies once). HP damage from each does stack.
  • If the targeted module is destroyed, the Leech self-destructs — "mission complete" — with VFX/audio and no AOE damage.
Why position-driven, not random Players can angle their ship so missiles strike bare hull sections — and earn hull-chewer outcomes (slower, recoverable damage) instead of module-biters for their flying. Position-driven behavior also reads correctly: the Leech that lands next to your engines is going for the engines.
Concurrency safety rail Total active Leeches on the player ship are capped at 8 simultaneously. When the cap is hit, subsequent missile impacts play VFX and consume the projectile, but spawn no Leeches (in-fiction excuse: hull is saturated with EM noise). This prevents runaway swarms from soft-bricking a run.

EVA Removal

Leeches are EVA-only. Inside the ship, there is no way to engage them in v1. A crewmember suits up, exits the airlock, mag-walks to the Leech, aims the existing multi-tool, and holds it on the target for 3–5 seconds (scales with DifficultyScalar). The Leech detaches and dies. Done.

No mini-game, no input prompts, no escape mechanic. The cost is the EVA itself — leaving your station, traversing the hull, standing still and exposed while you hold the trigger.

Why EVA-only The identity of the encounter is "stop what you're doing, suit up, and go outside." Watering that down — letting people shoot Leeches through the hull from internal turrets — collapses the design into "another HP bar that happens to render on the hull." The EVA commitment is the design.

Sector Escalation Tie-In

The Upgrade Forge's ForgeMeterController.DifficultyScalar increments by +1 per sector jump. This RFC adds the Leech Carrier encounter to the things that scale with it:

  • Gating. Leech Missiles only appear at DifficultyScalar ≥ 2. Players have a full sector to learn the rest of the game before this archetype lands.
  • Encounter scaling. Leeches per missile, missile turn rate / HP / speed, host fire interval, removal time, and damage rates all scale beyond the gate — see Scaling Reference.
  • Tunable. All scaling values live in TerminusConfig and are tunable without rebuilding.

Crew Awareness

Awareness is a crew communication problem, by design — no bespoke alert popup, no hull-status console.

  • Visual awareness reuses existing tools: telescope module, 3rd-person cockpit, gunner external view.
  • Audio cues are diegetic — bite/chomp sounds travel through the hull and can be heard from inside the affected section.
  • One global sting: the first Leech of an encounter triggers a subtle ship-wide hull-resonance hum so idle crew know it has started. After that, audio is purely diegetic.

A Sample Encounter

At DifficultyScalar 4, mid-fight in sector 5:

T+0s — Inbound

The pilot, watching from 3rd-person, sees a familiar frigate. A long oddly-colored missile detaches, audibly warbling. Vanilla projectile warning pings. Pilot calls "Leech missile, port side." Gunner pivots — too slow. Point-defense lands a hit, but the missile keeps coming. Impact.

T+3s — Spawn

Hull-wide resonance hum. The missile bursts and deploys 2 Leeches. One lands beside the starboard engine — locks on. The other lands on an exposed hull section near the medbay — no module nearby, starts chewing the plate. Engines drop to 60% thrust. From inside the medbay, the crewmate hears a slow metallic chomp from above her head.

T+15s — Triage

Captain calls it: "Engine Leech first — we lose thrust we lose the fight." Crewmate suits up, exits the rear airlock, mag-walks to the engine Leech. Aims the multi-tool, holds. Four seconds later, pop. Engines snap back to 100%.

T+30s — Hull

She jogs across the hull to the second Leech. The chomp cadence is louder now — she can hear it through her suit. Holds the multi-tool. Pop. Hull section stops bleeding at 40%. "Hull clear." She heads back to the airlock. Total downtime: ~30 seconds, no module lost, no breach. Engines and hull will need a small repair pass after the fight.

Constants (do not scale):

  • Concurrent-Leech cap (8)
  • Self-destruct on targeted-module destruction
  • Position-driven module-vs-hull behavior threshold

Open Questions

  • Which vanilla enemy class is the Host? Pre-flight decompile to identify a mid-tier frigate that already appears in deeper sectors.
  • Does vanilla point-defense detect custom projectiles? If yes, free integration. If no, register the Leech Missile as a valid PD target.
  • Does the vanilla projectile-warning system pick up custom projectiles? Affects whether the "incoming missile" alert fires automatically.
  • Multi-tool hold gesture. Does the multi-tool already have a "hold to do thing" gesture we can hook, or do we need a new interaction state?
  • Concurrency cap value. 8 is the starter — playtest may push it up or down.

Risks & Mitigations

RiskMitigation
Encounter trivialized by PD-heavy builds — missile never lands. Per-scalar missile HP and speed scaling. Late-sector missiles slip through PD often. If still trivialized after playtest, bump scaling more aggressively.
Encounter punishing for solo / low-headcount crews — pilot can't both fly and EVA. Encounter intentionally rewards crew. Solo players will lose modules and take hull damage at deep sectors — a known cost of playing solo. Sector gating lets solos extract earlier.
Leeches stacking on the same module → module destroyed before crew can EVA. Non-stacking debuff multiplier. HP damage still stacks, but at a recoverable rate.
Concurrency cap (8) makes the encounter trivial late-run by tanking impacts. Each missile still consumes a projectile slot — absorbing 8 Leeches' worth requires several impacts. If exploit emerges, reduce cap or add slow cap-recovery.
EVA traversal slow → encounter drags combat into a 60–90s tail. Per-scalar fire interval ensures encounters don't overlap. Hold-to-remove is short (3–5s). Mag-boot traversal speed is a vanilla knob.
Multiplayer desync — clients see Leeches in different states. Host-arbitrated state, mirroring the Upgrade Forge pattern. All RNG host-side; clients replay.

Alternatives Considered

New enemy ship type as Host

Design a brand-new ship — model, AI, spawn registration. Rejected — the repo has no enemy authoring infrastructure yet; building it on top of the Leech feature triples scope. A patched vanilla class is enough.

Distinct A-type / B-type Leech variants

Two named variants (Module-Biter and Hull-Biter) with separate behavior tuning. Rejected — the variant distinction can be expressed as a single behavior driven by spawn position (module nearby vs. not), saving a parallel authoring track for very little additional game-feel gain.

Containment input prompts during removal

Periodic on-screen prompts during the hold; missing one lets the Leech escape and re-anchor. Rejected — adds a QTE loop and a chase loop on top of the EVA. The EVA itself is already the commitment; layering a mini-game inside it dilutes the focus and roughly doubles the encounter timeline.

Internal destruction (kill Leeches with internal turrets)

Let players engage Leeches through the hull from inside. Rejected for v1 — collapses the EVA-centric identity. Captured as a future variant for a deeper-sector "armored" Leech.

Dedicated EVA tool (Leech Pry-Bar)

Author a new equippable specifically for Leech removal. Rejected for v1 — the existing multi-tool is enough. Captured as a future variant.

Bespoke hull-status UI / alert popups

Mod-side UI showing all attached Leech positions. Rejected — telescope, cockpit, and turret external views already exist. Crew awareness should reuse those, not be overridden by a mod-side cheat sheet.

Future Variants

Captured during design but out of scope for v1.

  • Internal-destructible armored Leeches — deep-sector subtype shootable through the hull with internal turrets, at a steep cost (chip damage to your own hull).
  • Dedicated removal tool — a Leech Pry-Bar equippable that removes faster.
  • Crawling Leeches — procedural spider-walk across the hull, either random-walk or seeking the EVA crew. Requires hull-surface pathing.
  • Escape-and-re-anchor — Leeches break containment under input pressure during removal, scurrying to a new location. Adds drama once core encounter is locked.
  • Defensive lunge — Leeches snap at the EVA crewmember during removal, chip-damaging the suit. Requires the crawling foundation.
  • Burrowing subtype — fleeing Leeches penetrate the hull and become internal pests requiring a different removal mechanic.
  • Custom audio / visual polish pass — bespoke Leech model, missile model, VFX, SFX. v1 ships with primitives.
