Upgrade Forge — RFC

RFC VoidCrewTerminus mod Design only

Design-level proposal for the Upgrade Forge — a new ship module that extends module progression past level 3 up to level 10, using relics as the upgrade input. For the technical companion, see upgrade-forge-design.html.

Summary

The Upgrade Forge is a new installable ship module that lets a crew push their weapons, shields, reactors, and other modules past the L3 ceiling, up to L10. Upgrading consumes relics inserted into the Forge in-world. Some relics are cursed — they upgrade just fine, but the module they touch becomes annoying to operate. The system layers on top of vanilla Void Crew without changing what already works.

Motivation

Today's late run hits a ceiling fast. Once modules are L3 and the hold is full of relics, there's not much left to invest in. Relics become "items I pick up to craft a thing later" instead of progression in their own right. The TODO file at the project root names the gap: "new leveling system for weapons; lean into the roguelike aspects; levels 4–10 use relics to upgrade."

This RFC turns that note into a system. The aim is to:

  • Give the late run something meaningful to fight for.
  • Make relics feel like loot worth hunting.
  • Add risk/reward via cursed relics — the cost is operational, not statistical.
  • Keep the runs themselves clean — no meta-progression, no permanent unlocks.

Goals & Non-Goals

Goals

  • Extend module progression to L10 in a way that feels earned.
  • Make individual relic drops matter — "do I curse my favorite turret with this?"
  • Preserve the existing roguelike loop: everything resets when the ship dies.
  • Don't break vanilla. L1–L3 module behavior is untouched.

Non-Goals

  • No meta-progression. No persistent levels across runs. No save format change.
  • No new currencies. Only existing relics are used as upgrade input.
  • No replacement of the BuildBox / module-swap upgrade flow. Vanilla L1→L2→L3 is untouched.
  • No new enemies, biomes, or POIs in this RFC.

Module Levels (3 → 10)

Today every module caps at L3. This proposal adds L4 through L10, accessible only through the Upgrade Forge. Vanilla L1→L3 progression still works as it does — when crew finish that, they bring the L3 module to the Forge to push further.

Each level above 3 is a small, deterministic stat bump (damage, fire rate, output, capacity) and may roll a single perk — a flat modifier drawn from the module's category pool (weapons, reactors, shields, engines, scanners). A module gets at most one perk over its lifetime. The roll happens on first eligible upgrade (L4+) and either lands or doesn't; later upgrades don't re-roll.

Stats are tuned so an L10 module is meaningfully stronger than an L3, but not so strong that L4–L6 feel like wasted intermediate steps.

The Forge

The Upgrade Forge is a new ship module the crew installs like any other. It has one interaction surface: relic slots. Crew carry relics to it and insert them.

The Forge's own level determines its Capacity — how many relics it can hold at once, which caps the upgrade level you can attempt (L10 needs 4 relics inserted simultaneously, so you need a Forge that holds 4). The Forge gains capacity passively with each successful sector jump, growing in lockstep with Sector Escalation.

Upgrading a module: walk relics to the Forge, target a module, commit. Relics are consumed. The module gains a level — and on the first eligible upgrade, may roll a perk.

Diegetic, not menu-driven Insertion is in-world — you physically carry a relic to the Forge, you physically put it in. There's no inventory menu for the relic side.
Perk is committed first, revealed after You don't see what perk you're getting before committing. You insert relics, you commit, you live with the result. This preserves the "did I just find a treasure?" tension a preview would dissolve.

Cursed Relics

Roughly 30% of relics are cursed. A cursed relic still works — the stat bump is full strength, the perk roll still happens — but if a perk lands, it carries a Maintenance Burden.

Burden is operational, not statistical A burdened module is not weaker on paper. It just becomes annoying to operate. It might cut out briefly at random. It might radiate heat to neighbors. It might require a manual reset every so often. The cost shows up as crew work, not as a tooltip debuff.

Example perks (illustrative — final values during content authoring):

PerkBenefitBurden
Overcharged+30% damagePowers off for 2–4s every 30–90s
Vampiric5% damage healed to ship hullRadiates heat; nearby modules tick warmer
Erratic+50% fire rate~5% chance per shot to require a manual reset

The intent is emergent gameplay. A crew with three Overcharged turrets is choosing chaos — when the swarm shows up, someone is running ladder-to-ladder reactivating things.

Sector Escalation

Each successful sector jump scales the run in two ways:

  • Enemy threat rises — HP, damage, density.
  • Forge Capacity rises — deeper jumps let you commit to bigger upgrades.

Sector progression is the only knob — there's no separate alloy investment loop. The Forge grows with the run; how far you push the run determines how far you can push your modules. That makes "do we jump again or extract?" a real decision.

A Sample Run

To make this concrete, here's how a run might unfold with the system in place.

Sector 1 — Departure

You install the Upgrade Forge in the back bay before launch. Capacity 1. Your starboard turret is already L3 from the BuildBox kit. No relics yet.

Sector 2 — First relic

Looting a derelict, you find a relic. You carry it back to the Forge, target the starboard turret, and commit. Turret goes L3→L4. Damage ticks up. No perk this time.

Sector 4 — Perk roll

Forge Capacity is now 2. You push the starboard turret to L5 with a single relic — and this time it rolls "Hyperflux," a small reload-speed bonus. The turret's only perk slot is now spent for the run; future upgrades just add stat.

Sector 5 — The cursed one

You find a cursed relic. It glows red. You hesitate — but the turret's perk slot is already filled by a clean perk, so a cursed relic here only delivers the stat bump. You commit it. L5→L6, no burden risk. (Had you used it earlier, before Hyperflux, the perk roll could have landed cursed.)

Sector 7 — The L10

By sector 7 the Forge Capacity is 4. You've been hoarding. Four relics into the Forge, target the starboard turret, commit. The turret hits L10. It's terrifying. The other modules are still L3.

Sector 9 — Death

A bad jump, a swarm you can't burn down. Ship dies. All of it — the L10 turret, the Forge, the relic stash — wipes. Next run starts fresh. That's the deal.

16 relics total to fully push one module from L3 to L10. A typical run yields fewer than 16 relics, which means crews must choose which module to commit to. That's the design.

Open Questions

Deferred to playtesting rather than locked at design time.

  • Cursed ratio. 30% is the starter. Could be 20% (curses are special) or 50% (curses are the norm).
  • Forge visual identity. Footprint, animation, particle effects.
  • Burden taxonomy. "Random shutoffs" is the seed; other event types authored alongside the cursed perks.
  • Player communication. How does the player know a module is L7 vs L10? Visible glyphs? Glowing intensity? Tooltip on look?
  • Capacity gate UX. When the Forge is Capacity 2 and the player tries an L10 upgrade, what does the rejection look like?
  • Multiplayer feel. When a crewmate commits an upgrade, do all players see the same reveal animation?

Risks & Mitigations

RiskMitigation
L10 modules trivialize the endgame. Cost ramp (16 relics for one module) and tight relic supply mean one or two L10s per great run, not five. Sector Escalation also pushes enemy difficulty.
Cursed relics feel mandatory — the bonus is so good players always take the burden. Make burdens genuinely costly in active gameplay. If still mandatory after playtest, reduce the stat bonus or make the burden more disruptive.
Cursed relics feel like trash — the burden is so bad nobody uses them. If playtest shows curse-avoidance, sweeten the bonus (e.g. cursed relics always roll a perk; clean ones might not).
Players hoard relics for the perfect upgrade and never commit during a run. Forge Capacity is limited early — holding doesn't help if you can't insert yet. Combined with per-run reset, hoarding is actively bad.
Multiplayer feels desynced — clients see different module states. Host is the sole authority on rolls; clients receive results. (Implementation detail in the technical doc.)

Alternatives Considered

Extend the existing Fabricator tier system to L10

Raise the Fabricator's cap. Rejected — that upgrades the crafting machine, not the modules it produces. Weapons would still be L1/L2/L3 prefab swaps; nothing about the ship modules would change.

Author 7 new prefabs per weapon (L4–L10 variants)

Rejected for content scope — that's 7 × (every weapon, reactor, shield) new assets, plus balancing each. Own multi-month project.

Meta-progression (modules persist across runs)

An L10 turret you keep forever. Rejected — dilutes the roguelike loop. Void Crew's identity is "everything dies when the ship dies."

Tiered relics (Common / Rare / Legendary)

Add a quality dial on top of the relic system, with tiered perk slots gated by relic tier. Rejected for scope — multiplies the design surface (drop rates, tier-aware perk pools, balancing across rarity), and the single-perk-slot model already captures the "this relic matters" feeling without the bookkeeping.

Alloy-fed Forge Meter

Let crews accelerate Forge progression by spending alloys at a terminal. Rejected — added a second economy alongside relics for marginal benefit. Passive growth tied to sector jumps is simpler and reinforces the "push deeper" decision instead of dividing player attention.

Visible perk preview before committing

Show three possible perks, let the player pick one. Rejected — removes the gamble. We want "what did I just get?" not "what should I shop for?"

A custom upgrade currency separate from relics

Introduce a new pickup for L4–L10. Rejected — crowds the loot table and adds a second economy without enriching the existing one.
